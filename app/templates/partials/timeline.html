1977

James Brzyski is ordained.

1977-1981

Brzyski is the assistant pastor St. John the Evangelist, Lower Makefield, Bucks County.

1981-1984

Brzyski is the associate pastor, St. Cecilia’s, Northeast Philadelphia.

1984

Archdiocese of Philadelphia removes Brzyski from St. Cecilia’s after he admits to having sexually molested boys from the Bucks County parish. Is sent to a Catholic treatment facility, where he is declared a pedophile.

1985

Brzyski walks out on treatment and leaves ministry. Archdiocese urges him to remain a priest. Brzyski refuses and walks away. Archdiocese does not notify law enforcement or parishioners about his alleged crimes.

1985 or 1986

Brzyski took a high school teaching job in the Diocese of Metuchen, N.J.

1987-1992

Brzyski owned a house on the 1200 block of Panama Street in Center City, Philadelphia.

1993-2000

Brzyski owned a house on the 3400 block of Midvale Avenue in East Falls, Philadelphia; ran a child’s birthday party business.

2002-2007

Brzyski lived in Virginia. In 2002 was arrested on charges of molesting a teenager. The case was dismissed.

Sometime between 2002 and 2005

Brzyski appeared before Philadelphia grand jury investigating archdiocesan clergy abuse. Refused to answer questions about whether he molested possibly more than 100 kids.

2005

Brzyski was defrocked by Catholic Church. Grand jury report unmasks him as one of 63 priests credibly accused of sexually abusing children in five-county Philadelphia archdiocese.

2009

Brzyski lived in West Hollywood, CA.

2010

Brzyski was arrested for drunken driving in New Mexico; had a court case and residential address in Kenosha, Wisconsin.

2014

Brzyski was run out of a Dallas residence after neighbors realized his past as an alleged pedophile ex-priest. Moved to a different Dallas apartment complex.

September 2017

Brzyski died in a Fort Worth, Texas, hotel room three weeks after his Dallas neighbors learned from reporters of Brzyski’s past as an abuser priest.
